About Us

Ever since they first used Adam’s mother’s camcorder to shoot a poorly budgeted Spaghetti Western, Adam and Darian have teamed up to do what they love: making videos. While attending different high schools across the city, they would come together on weekends and make short films, both comedic and serious, to satisfy their thirst for filmmaking.

Over the past years, they’ve worked for the National Energy Foundation on educational films shown to students around the country. They have made commercials for Casio. They have directed a short film for the National Institute of Health as part of a grant to increase organ donation. They have won awards both statewide and nationally for suicide awareness and energy consciousness. But when it comes down to it, they just enjoy being on all sides of the camera to make their visions, no matter how dark or silly, a reality.

Who we are

Adam Hersko-RonaTas

Born in San Diego, CA, Adam was raised in the lovely neighborhood of North Park. He is currently a senior at La Jolla High School where he is the commissioner for video in the Associated Student Body. He is fluent in both English and Hungarian. His sports of choice: waterpolo and swim team. He has worked with videos since age 10 and has grown to appreciate it more each year.

Darian Spencer

Darian has been interested in film since age 11. He attended San Diego High School of International studies where he received the IB diploma and participated in the IB film course. He is fluent in English and German. In 2010 he attended a high school in Frankfurt for three months. His hobbies include swimming, drawing, biking, and drumming. He will be attending the University of Colorado Boulder.
